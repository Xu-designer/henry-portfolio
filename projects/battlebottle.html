BATTLE BOTTLE

For this assignment in DES 014, we were to reinvent a paper sculpture of our choice into a design object, and represent it on a 11x14 sheet of paper. We were also to inspire the design after an illustrator's style of our choice, based on the research done in a previous assignment.

PAPER SCULPTURE

The image above shows the paper sculpture I chose. With its long, twisting form and pointed end, this sculpture reminded me of the bullet-shaped bottle my family had gotten recently from a military attraction. This served as the inspiration for what was to be the Battle Bottle.

ILLUSTRATOR INSPIRATION

The designer I ended up taking inspiration from for this project was Ken Sugimori, a major designer and illustrator for Pokemon and other works. His early illustratons used a watercolor style with high contrast lighting, which matched well with my own intention to use watercolor.

INITIAL IDEATION

This is the first iteration of my design, which I ended up changing a lot. This version doesn't put as much focus on the bottle itself, and the figure is facing away from the front. I decided for my final design, it needed to put central focus on the design object.
